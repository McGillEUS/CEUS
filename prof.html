Social Events

We host many events throughout the year to get everyone out of their textbooks. Events range from apartment crawls, camping, and banquets!

Academics

CEUS is the liaison between the department and students. We organizes academic events such as Speaker Series with Professors and provides academic support. We also gather course feedback, decide course funding and help students navigate the curriculum

Career Development

Through the academic year, CEUS and CSCE host industry tours and networking Events. Students witness and learn about the various career paths their degree can lead them to.

Meet Prof Andrew Boyd

Prof Andrew Boyd is one of the Structural professor in the civil department

Basic

What classes do you teach?

CIVE 202, CIVE 527, CIVE 623

Your favourite class to teach and why?

CIVE 202 - It's fun to introduce students to the technical details of materials they have literally seen every day of their lives, usually without really considering them. I always get some very interesting comments as students realize just how complex these everyday materials really are.

What tech comp do you think all civil students should take and why?

CIVE 527 - because I teach it. No ... because they get to go out in the field and see just how badly deteriorated many of our structures actualy are. It's really an eye-opener for many of them.

How can students best succeed in your classes?

Attend class and stay awake.

What were your favourite and least favourite classes?

Favourite - Construction Materials. Least - Structural Analysis.

What is your favourite thing about your job?

The responses/comments I get from students who really enjoy learning, especially when they have one of those 'WHOA' moments while learning something about a material that is completely opposite to what they previosuly believed - which is very common with concrete in particular.

Meet Prof Colin Rogers

Prof Rogers is one of the Structural professor in the civil department

What classes do you teach?

CIVE 318 Structural Engineering II and CIVE 462 Design of Steel Structures

Your favourite class to teach and why?

CIVE 462 - get the chance to look at design using a full building

There is no one course that all students should take. In your final year it is time to decide on which disciplines within Civil Engineering are most interesting, and make your choice of 400 tech comp courses based on this.

How can students best succeed in your classes?

Know your statics. Know the design procedures inside out and backwards. Write the procedures in your own words.

What were your favourite and least favourite classes?

Favourite - steel and matrix

What is your favourite thing about your job?

Running full-scale tests in the lab
