Extracurricular

McGill Civil Engineering students don’t spend all their time in classrooms or nose-deep in textbooks and assignments. Instead, we in-volve ourselves in various extracurricular activities that range from attending meet-and-greets with professional engineers to building a concrete canoe (yes, we said concrete ca-noe!) These activities provide us with several opportunities to gain hands-on experience within the  eld of engineering and to network with professionals already in the  eld. On top of that, they are really fun!

Canadian Society for Civil Engineering (CSCE) McGill

The McGill Chapter of the Canadian Society for Civil En-gineering (CSCE) links students to the world of civil en-gineering. They organize various events which include tours to construction sites, special guest talks, and meet-and-greets with engineering professionals. We also host events in collaboration with other universities across Quebec.

Concrete Canoe

The Concrete Canoe Team designs and builds a canoe made entirley of concrete. Members are able to contrib-ute to many di erent aspects of the project, including: designing the hull shape in AutoCAD, doing structural analysis in MATLAB, designing and mixing lightweight concrete, designing aesthetic enhancements, building the canoe and even paddling. The Concrete Canoe Team competes annually in the national competition in May.

Engineers in Action

At McGill, we not only learn to design a suspension bridge, but we also fundraise the money necessary to cover all the project expenses. Our team is also involved in the project implementation with the construction of the bridge during the summer.We empower our students to become global leaders while connecting underserved communities!

Bridge Building

Are you interested in making a 1-meter long bridge, only out of popsicle sticks, white glue and floss, that can withstand more than 1000 pounds? In McGill’s Bridge Building Team, we do just that. Every year, schools from around Canada and the United States use their engineering skills to build popsicle-stick bridges and compete against each other in the Troitsky Bridge Building Competition.
